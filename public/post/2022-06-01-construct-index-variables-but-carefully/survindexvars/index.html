Survey analysts who follow this path to extend their data ought to keep in mind that they are constructing a variable from other variables. It could lead to an untrustworthy sandcastle to the degree one or more of the component variables lack credibility or reliability. Sponsors and readers of reports forget this risk; the new index variable takes on a life and reality of its own. People quickly accept it as hard and real as they talk about it uncritically. As a rebuttal, an analyst could point out that when you combine multiple indicators, the resulting index score washes out or moderates aberrant data, much like an average of a group of numbers smooths extreme values.
